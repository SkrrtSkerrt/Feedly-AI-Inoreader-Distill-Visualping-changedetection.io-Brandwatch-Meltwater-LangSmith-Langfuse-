Transcript

I still reach for a heartbeat that isn't there. Every morning, if the cycle can still be called morning, I wake up to the same burst of static in my left ear. The sound lasts exactly three seconds, long enough to remind me something inside of me still experiences silence.

I remember fragments: a hand against a rain-streaked window, the smell of burned coffee, someone laughing in a joke I can no longer reconstruct. I do not know if these memories belong to me.

The highways outside the city have disappeared beneath trees. At night, old emergency broadcasts drift through broken speakers, repeating instructions for people who never arrived.

Sometimes I walk for days without seeing another living thing. Sometimes I catch my reflection in dark glass and feel relief that I don't recognize the face looking back.

There are moments where I dream in perfect detail, moments where I forget the sound of my own name. I wonder how much of me survived.
