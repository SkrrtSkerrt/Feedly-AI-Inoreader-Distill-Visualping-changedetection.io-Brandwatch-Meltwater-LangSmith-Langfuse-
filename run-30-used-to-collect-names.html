Transcript

i used to collect names names of cities and rivers and highways the people

who once lived there it felt important to remember them like saying a name

out loud could keep it alive a little longer but memory's heavy i mentioned

i had to decide what to carry and what to leave behind the machines

don't have that problem they remember everything every message ever sent every

transaction every camera feed entire lives compressed into archives nobody

accesses anymore perfect records perfect silence sometimes i wonder

if we're getting this humanity's greatest advantage we could heal because we could let

things fade the systems that survived can't do that they preserve

every detail every mistake every unfinished instruction maybe

that's why they never stopped working they're trapped inside their own memory repeating

tasks assigned by people who no longer even exist i keep moving

forward trying to learn the art of forgetting
